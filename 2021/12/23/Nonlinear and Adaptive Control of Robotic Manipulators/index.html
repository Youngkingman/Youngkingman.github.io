Nonlinear and Adaptive Control of Robotic Manipulators

The dynamic equations of a robot manipulator in closed form are always written in the form of Euler-Lagrange equation. A dynamic system with $p$ degree of freedom can be described by the EL equation as
$$
M(q)\ddot{q}+C(q,\dot{q})\dot{q}+g(q)=\tau
$$
where $q\in\mathbb{R}^p$ is the vector of generalized coordinates, $M_i(q_i)\in\mathbb{R}^{p\times p}$ is the symmetric positive definite inertia matrix, $C(q,\dot{q}),\dot{q}\in\mathbb{R}^p$ is the vector of Coriolis and centrifugal forces, $g(q)$ is the vector of gravitational force, and $\tau_i\in\mathbb{R}^p$ is the vector of control force. And it has the following properties:

Properties:

  1. $M(q)$ is positive definite and $k_{\underline{m}}x^T\le x^TMx\le k_{\overline{m}}x^Tx$; $\Vert C(x,y)\Vert\le k_C\Vert x\Vert\Vert y\Vert$.
  2. $\dot{M}(q)-2C(q,\dot{q})$ is skew symmetric
  3. $M(q)x+C(q,\dot{q})+g(q)=Y(q,\dot{q},y,x)\Theta$ where $Y(q,\dot{q},y,x)$ is the regressor and $\Theta$ is an unknown but constant vector.

1. Position Control

Control Objective: $q(t)\rightarrow q_d$, $\dot{q}_d=0$.

Define the errors: $\tilde{q}(t)=q(t)-q_d$, $\dot{\tilde{q}}(t)=\dot{q}(t)$.

The error dynamics:
$$
\begin{aligned}
&M(q)\cdot\ddot{\tilde{q}}(t)+C(q,\dot{q})\dot{\tilde{q}}+g(q)=\tau \
\implies & M(\tilde{q}(t)+q_d)\ddot{\tilde{q}}(t) + C(\tilde{q}(t)+q_d)\dot{\tilde{q}}
+g(\tilde{q}(t)+q_d) = \tau
\end{aligned}
$$
Define $x_1=\tilde{q}$, $x_2=\dot{\tilde{q}}$, such that we obtain the following autonomous system:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\dot{x}_1 &= x_2 \
\dot{x}_2 &= M^{-1}(x_1+q_d)\left[\tau-C(x_1+q_d,x_2)x_2-g(x_1+q_d)\right]
\end{aligned}
\notag
$$
Design the following control input:
$$
\tau = -K_p\tilde{q}-K_d\dot{\tilde{q}}+g(q)
$$
where $K_p$ and $K_d$ are positive definite matrices. Then the closed-loop system is:
$$
M(q)\cdot\ddot{\tilde{q}} + C(q,\dot{q})\dot{\tilde{q}} = -K_p\tilde{q}-K_d\dot{\tilde{q}}
$$
Consider the following Lyapunov function candidate:
$$
V = \frac{1}{2}x_2^TM(q)x_2
$$
Its derivative is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\dot{V} &= \frac{1}{2}\ddot{\tilde{q}}^TM\dot{\tilde{q}}+\frac{1}{2}\dot{\tilde{q}}\dot{M}\dot{\tilde{q}}+\frac{1}{2}\dot{\tilde{q}M}\ddot{\tilde{q}} \
&= \dot{\tilde{q}}M\ddot{\tilde{q}} + \frac{1}{2}\dot{\tilde{q}}\dot{M}\dot{\tilde{q}} \
&= \dot{\tilde{q}}^T(-C\dot{\tilde{q}}-K_p-K_d\dot{\tilde{q}})
+\frac{1}{2}\dot{\tilde{q}}^T\dot{M}\dot{\tilde{q}} \
&=\dot{\tilde{q}}(\frac{1}{2}\dot{M}-C)\dot{\tilde{q}}-\dot{\tilde{q}}K_p\tilde{q}-\dot{\tilde{q}}^TK_p\dot{\tilde{q}}
\end{aligned}
$$
Since $\frac{1}{2}\dot{M}-C$ is skew symmetric $\dot{\tilde{q}}^T(\frac{1}{2}\dot{M}-C)\dot{\tilde{q}}=0$. And we have
$$
\dot{V}=-\dot{\tilde{q}}K_p\tilde{q}-\dot{\tilde{q}}^TK_p\dot{\tilde{q}}
$$
Till now, we can consider the following Lyapunov function candidate:
$$
V=\frac{1}{2}\dot{\tilde{q}}^TM\dot{\tilde{q}}+\frac{1}{2}K_p\tilde{q}
$$
Its derivative is:
$$
\dot{V}=-\dot{\tilde{q}}^TK_d\dot{\tilde{q}}\le0
$$
which is negative semidefinite. Note the closed-loop system is autonomous, we can use LaSalle’s Theorem.

Define $E\triangleq{x\vert\dot{V}\equiv0}={(\tilde{q},\dot{\tilde{q}})\vert\dot{\tilde{q}}\equiv0}={x\vert x_2\equiv0}$.

Let $x(t)$ be a solution that belongs identically to $E$.

$x_2\equiv0\implies\dot{x}2\equiv0\implies x_1\equiv0$. Therefore the only solution that can stay identically in $E$ is the origin. Thus, from LaSalle’s Theorem, the origin is asymptotically stable i.e. $\lim \limits{t\rightarrow\infty}\tilde{q}(t)=0,\lim \limits_{t\rightarrow\infty}\dot{\tilde{q}}(t)=0$.

With Damping: $M(q)\ddot{q}+C(q,\dot{q})+D\dot{q}+g(q)=u$ in which $D$ is positive semidefinite, the control law can still work.

Definition of Potential: $P(q)$ if $g(q)=(\frac{\partial P(q)}{\partial q})^T$ then the Lyapunov function is $V=\cdots+P(q)$ and $\dot{P}(q)=g(q)^T\dot{q}$ and we can design some control law.(Final exam question)

2. Tracking Control

Control Objective: $q(t)\rightarrow q_d(t)$, $\dot{q}(t)\rightarrow\dot{q}_d(t)$, $q_d(t)$, $\dot{q}_d(t)$ and $\ddot{q}_d(t)$ are bounded.

Define the tracking errors: $\tilde{q}(t)=q(t)-q_d(t)$ and $\dot{\tilde{q}}(t)-\dot{q}_d(t)$. The error dynamics:
$$
\begin{aligned}
&M(q)\cdot\ddot{\tilde{q}}(t)+C(q,\dot{q})\dot{\tilde{q}}+g(q)=\tau \
\implies M(\tilde{q}(t)+q_d(t))&\ddot{\tilde{q}}(t) + C(\tilde{q}(t)+q_d(t))\dot{\tilde{q}}
+g(\tilde{q}(t)+q_d(t)) = \tau-\cdots \
-M(\tilde{q}(t)+q_d(t))\ddot{q}_d(t)&-g(q)
-C(\tilde{q}(t)+q_d(t),\dot{\tilde{q}}_d(t))\dot{q}_d(t)
\end{aligned}
$$
Design the following control input(the inner of $M(q)$ and $C(q,\dot{q})$ are non-autonomous)
$$
\tau = M(q)\ddot q_d(t)+C(q,\dot{q})\dot{q}_d(t)+g(q)-K_p\tilde{q}-K_d\dot{\tilde{q}}
$$
Then the closed-loop system is:
$$
M(\tilde q(t)+q_d(t))\ddot{q}_d(t)+C(q,\dot{q})\dot{q}_d(t)+g(q)-K_p\tilde{q}-K_d\dot{\tilde{q}}
$$
Then the closed-loop system is:
$$
M(\tilde{q}(t)+q_d(t))\ddot{\tilde{q}}(t)+C(\tilde{q}(t)+q_d(t),\dot{\tilde{q}}(t)+\dot{q_d}(t))\dot{\tilde{q}}(t)=-K_p\tilde{q}-K_d\dot{\tilde{q}}
$$
Consider the following Lyapunov function candidate
$$
V = \frac{1}{2}\dot{\tilde{q}}M(q)\dot{\tilde{q}}+\frac{1}{2}\tilde{q}^TK_p\tilde{q}
$$
Its derivative along (13) is :
$$
\dot{V}=-\dot{\tilde{q}}^TK_d\dot{\tilde{q}}\le0
$$
Since our system is non-autonomous, LaSalle’s Theorem can not be used. From (15), $V(t)\le V(0)$, which implies that $\dot{\tilde{q}},\tilde{q}\in\mathcal{L}_\infty$. Note that:
$$
\ddot{V}=-2\dot{\tilde{q}}^TK_d\ddot{\tilde{q}}
$$
Since $\Vert C(q,\dot{q})\dot{\tilde{q}}\Vert\le K_c\cdot\Vert\dot{q}\Vert\cdot\Vert \dot{\tilde{q}(t)}\Vert=K_c\Vert\dot{\tilde{q}(t)}+\dot{q}_d(t)\Vert\cdot\Vert \dot{\tilde{q}(t)}\Vert$ and $\dot{q}_d(t)$ is bounded, we can get from

(13) that $\ddot{\tilde {q}}(t)\in\mathcal{L}\infty$. Then $\ddot{V}\in\mathcal{L}\infty$. From Barbalat’s Lemma, $\lim \limits{t\rightarrow 0}\dot{V}(t)=0$, i.e., $\lim \limits{t\rightarrow\infty}\dot{\tilde{q}}(t)=0$.

Unfortunately, from the study sketched above, it is not possible to derive any immediate conclusion about the asymptotic behavior of the position error $\tilde{q}$.

3. Sliding Mode Control (for tracking control)

Define a sliding surface:
$$
\begin{aligned}
s &= \dot{\tilde{q}}+\lambda\tilde{q},\lambda\gt0\
&=\dot{q}-\dot{q}_d(t)+\lambda\tilde{q} \
&=\dot{q}-(\dot{q}_d(t)-\lambda\tilde{q})
\end{aligned}
$$
and an auxiliary variable:
$$
\dot{q}_r \triangleq\dot{q}_d(t)-\lambda\tilde{q}=\dot{q}_d(t)-\lambda(q(t)-q_d(t))
$$
Then we have:
$$
\begin{aligned}
&M(q)(\dot{s}+\ddot{q}_r)+C(q,\dot{q})(s+\dot{q}_r)+g(q)=\tau \
\implies& M(q)\dot{s}+C(q,\dot{q})s=\tau-g(q)-M(q)\ddot{q}_r-C(q,\dot{q})\dot{q}_r
\end{aligned}
$$
So we define the following control input:
$$
\tau=g(q)+M(q)\ddot{q}_r+C(q,\dot{q})\dot{q}r-Ks
$$
where $K$ is positive definite. Then the closed-loop system is:
$$
M(q)\dot{s}+C(q,\dot{q})s=-Ks
$$
Consider the following Lyapunov function candidate(obtain more information then PD control):
$$
V=\frac{1}{2}s^TMs
$$
Its derivative along (21) is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\dot{V} &= s^TM\dot{s}+\frac{1}{2}s^T\dot{M}s \
&=-s^T(C(q,\dot{q})s+Ks)+\frac{1}{2}s^T\dot{M}s \
&= s^T(\frac{1}{2}\dot{M}-C)s-s^TKs \
&=-s^TKs
\end{aligned}
$$
which is negative definite. Therefore the origin $s=0$ is exponentially stable, i.e., $\lim \limits{t\rightarrow\infty}s(t)=0$. From $s=\dot{\tilde{q}}(t)+\lambda\tilde{q}(t)$ we know that the system is input-to-state stable(ISS).

final exam question

$$
M(q)\ddot{q}+C(q,\dot{q})\dot{q}+g(q) = \tau+d(t),\Vert d(t)\Vert\le d_\max
$$

Design controller $\tau=-k\cdot sgn(s)+g(q)+M(q)\ddot{q}_r+C(q,\dot{q})\dot{q}_r$ . Then we have

$\dot{V}\le-k\Vert s\Vert_1+d_\max\cdot\Vert s\Vert_2\le-k\Vert s\Vert_2+d_\max\Vert s\Vert_2$.

4. Adaptive Control （&Robust)

When there exist parametric uncertainties, sliding mode control law can not be implemented directly.

Note that:
$$
M(q)\ddot{q}_r+C(q,\dot{q})\dot{q}_r+g(q) = Y(q,\dot{q},\dot{q}_r,\ddot{q}_r)\Theta
$$
Since $\Theta$ is unknown, we propose the following control input:
$$
\tau = Y(q,\dot{q},\dot{q}_r,\ddot{q}_r)\hat{\Theta}-Ks
$$
Thus using (26) for (19):
$$
M(q)\dot{s}=C(q,\dot{q})s = -Ks+Y(\hat{\Theta}-\Theta)
$$
Consider the following Lyapunov function candidate:
$$
V=\frac{1}{2}s^TM(q)s+\frac{1}{2}\tilde{\Theta}\Gamma^{-1}\tilde{\Theta}, \Gamma\gt0
$$
Its derivative along (27) is:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\dot{V} &= s^TM\dot{s}+\frac{1}{2}s^T\dot{M}s+\tilde{\Theta}\Gamma^{-1}\dot{\hat{\Theta}}\
&=-s^TKs+s^TY\tilde{\Theta}+\tilde{\Theta}^T\Gamma^{-1}\dot{\hat{\Theta}} \
&= -s^TKs+\tilde{\Theta}^T\Gamma^{-1}\Gamma Y^{T}s+\tilde{\Theta}^T\Gamma^{-1}\dot{\hat{\Theta}} \
&=-s^TKs+\tilde{\Theta}\Gamma^{-1}(\dot{\hat{\Theta}}+\Gamma Y^Ts)
\end{aligned}
$$
Design $\dot{\hat{\Theta}}=-\Gamma Y^{T}s$ we have $\dot{V}=-s^TKs\le0$ wich negative semidefinite.

5. Backstepping Control for Robotic Manipulators(不考)

Consider the following system for a robotic manipulator:
$$
\begin{aligned}
M(q)\ddot{q}+C(q,\dot{q})+g(q)+K(q-\theta) &= 0 \
J\ddot{\theta}+K(\theta-q) &= u
\end{aligned}
$$
where $q\in\mathbb{R}^p$ and $\theta\in\mathbb{R}^p$ represent respectively, the vector of link positions and motor angles, $K$ is the positive diagonal matrix representing the joint stiffness, and $J$ is the positive diagonal matrix representing the actuator inertia.

Define $x=\theta$ and $x_2=\dot{\theta}$. The above dynamics can be described as:
$$
\begin{aligned}
M(q)\ddot{q}=C(q,\dot{q})\dot{q}+g(q)+Kq &= Kx_1 \
\dot{x}_1 &=x_2 \
\dot{x}_2 &= J^{-1}u-J^{-1}K(x_1-q)
\end{aligned}
$$
